Existing Problem Statements:

Can we causally understand the reasons of unemployment in an economy? Can medical knowledge lead to a higher number of visit to doctors/ hospitals? Can we expect the dropout rates to come down if the future career path information is made readily available? Or, can we reliably predict/estimate skill requirement/ employment growth in the economy? How can we validate such predictions? Can we predict poverty at the household level without using survey? Can we do it sustainably and at a real time manner? — are some of the research problems we are working upon.

Some tidbits on methodology:

We are working at the intersection of statistics, machine learning and Bayesian network. Both Statistical/ Econometric Method and the Machine Learning Methods have their own advantages/ disadvantages. We work to use the available tools from these two domains in specific problems. Whereas, we are mindful about using statistical and ML tools, our core emphasis is on the application of Bayesian Network to bring the causality aspects. — An illustration of these applications may be found in our research on modeling unemployment in India. One of our core task is to validate the dataset, thus generated, using traditional methods. In other words, we explore the levels of accuracy, cost-efficiency, reliability of big datasets thus generated using alternative methodologies. —- An illustration of this application may be found in our research on estimating poverty using alternative datasets (day light satellite images, image collection using Drone etc) A critical aspect of our work is data generation. We deploy innovative methods to generate our own data for the research. We deploy drone to collect high resolution images. We also develop web portals and make it available for public use. These portals , drones, and other similar interventions provide us necessary “passive” data for research.

Dataset:

We are using different datasets like text data, images, search behavior, and other datasets coming out of human actions. We prefer to use “passive data” i.e. datasets which are being generated without an active intervention of the researchers or the influence of researchers on the subject isn’t there. Essentially, we aim at generating datasets free of researcher/ surveyor bias or respondents bias. We intend the data collection method be automated so that we can obtain real time data without creating a long time lag between survey, data generation, and research usage. Finally, we intend to make sure the reliability/ accuracy/ usability of the datasets before using it for the research. We validate these datasets using existing survey data, conducting extensive survey at a shorter scale, and other means. Finally, we expect to collect data in a sustainable and real-time manner at a low cost.
